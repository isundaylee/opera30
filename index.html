Opera of the Future 30

In celebration of the 30th anniversary of the MIT Media Lab, Tod Machover set out to discover the sound(s) of his Opera of the Future group past and present. 30 sounds from 30 alumni over the past 30 years transformed into 3 x 30-second vignettes by Tod and his current students. 30-30-30.

Tod asked 30 Opera of the Future alumni to create a personal sound from 1-to-30 seconds representing how they hear the world and reflecting something about their experience at – and feeling about – the Media Lab. The video to the right is a compilation of 1-second excerpts from each of the submitted sounds.

Tod handed over all of the alumni sounds over to his current Opera of the Future students to hack, mix, and match. They analyzed the audio, devised ways identify the differences and pull out the similarities across the sounds, and mapped these parameters to physical devices which they “played” with Tod captured in this 30-second whirlwind video.

As the final homage to Opera of the Future past and present, Tod and his current students sat down in the studio to make an extra special mix of all of the alumni sounds received.
